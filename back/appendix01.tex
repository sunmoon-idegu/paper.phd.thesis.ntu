\appendix{A}{Validity of Branching Probabilities}\label{appendix:validity_of_p}

   To prove $P_u$, $P_m$, and $P_d$ are valid probabilities, it suffices to prove
    $P_u, P_m, P_d \geq 0$. We know $\Delta$ is negative in formula~(\ref{eq:det}). 
    This means $\Delta_u$, $\Delta_m$, and $\Delta_d$ in 
    formulas~(\ref{eq:detu})--(\ref{eq:detd}) must be non-positive or, equivalently,
    $\beta\gamma  + \text{Var} \geq 0$, $\alpha\gamma + \text{Var} \leq 0$, and
    $\alpha\beta  + \text{Var} \geq 0$. From 
    formulas~(\ref{eq:detu})--(\ref{eq:detd}) and 
    inequalities~(\ref{eq:beta_range}), we have
    \begin{align*}
        \beta\gamma  + \text{Var}\quad  &=\quad \beta^2 - 2\beta\sigma\sqrt{\Delta t} + \sigma^2\Delta t \\
                                        &=\quad \left(\beta - 2\sigma\sqrt{\Delta t}\right)^2 \geq 0, \numberthis \label{eq:appexdixA1} \\
        \alpha\gamma + \text{Var}\quad  &=\quad \beta^2 - 4\sigma^2\Delta t + \sigma^2\Delta t \\
                                        &=\quad \beta^2 - 3\sigma^2\Delta t \leq 0, \numberthis \label{eq:appexdixA2} \\
        \alpha\beta  + \text{Var}\quad  &=\quad \beta^2 + 2\beta\sigma\sqrt{\Delta t} + \sigma^2\Delta t \\
                                        &=\quad \left(\beta + 2\sigma\sqrt{\Delta t}\right)^2 \geq 0 \numberthis \label{eq:appexdixA3},
    \end{align*}
    as desired (see~\cite{BTT}).
